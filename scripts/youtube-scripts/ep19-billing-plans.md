# Episode 19 — Billing, Plans & Your Account
**Duration target:** 4–5 min
**Style:** Clear and honest — treat the viewer as a professional making a purchasing decision. No sales pressure; just a clean walkthrough of what each plan gets you, how the pricing works, where to find your usage, and how to upgrade. The per-event credit option is an important differentiator for occasional users — give it proper space.

---

## INTRO (0:00–0:30)

[Screen: CueDeck console, director view. The user profile chip is visible in the top-right of the header — showing the user's name and a role-coloured avatar.]

> "CueDeck has a free plan, a Pro plan, and an Enterprise option. It also has something a bit unusual: a per-event credit system for production teams who run events occasionally rather than continuously.
>
> In this episode I'll walk through all four options — what you get, what the limits are, when each one makes sense — and I'll show you where to find your current usage and how to upgrade directly from the console."

---

## THE PLAN OVERVIEW (0:30–1:30)

[Screen: Open a browser tab to cuedeck.io/pricing — or navigate to app.cuedeck.io and open the user profile panel. Show the pricing tiers cleanly on screen.]

> "There are three subscription plans and one credit option.
>
> Free gives you one active event at a time, up to five operators, two signage displays. No AI features, no post-event reports. It's enough to run your first event and evaluate whether CueDeck fits your workflow. No credit card required to sign up.
>
> Pro is €79 per month. Unlimited events, up to twenty operators, unlimited signage displays, full AI agent access — Incident Advisor, Cue Engine, Report Generator — and post-event report exports. This is the plan for teams running regular events: conferences, summits, roadshows, anything recurring.
>
> Enterprise is custom pricing. Unlimited operators, dedicated support, custom onboarding, SLA agreements, and volume invoicing. If you're running large-scale events with 50-person production crews or you need a contract and procurement process, Enterprise is the path."

[Screen: Show the pricing page or plan comparison table clearly. Highlight the Pro column.]

> "And then there's Per-Event credit. This is for the production professional who runs two or three events a year — maybe a freelancer, maybe a team that handles occasional corporate events. You buy a single event credit, it unlocks Pro features for one event, and when that event ends the credit is consumed. No monthly commitment."

---

## THE USER PROFILE PANEL (1:30–2:20)

[Screen: Click the name chip in the top-right of the CueDeck header. The user profile panel slides open — showing: plan badge (e.g. "FREE" or "PRO"), events used bar (e.g. "1 / 1 events"), sessions bar if applicable, and a Billing button at the bottom.]

> "Your current plan status is always one click away — in the user profile panel. Click your name chip in the header.
>
> At the top of the panel: your name, your role, and a plan badge. If you're on Free it shows FREE in grey. Pro shows PRO in blue. Enterprise shows ENTERPRISE in purple.
>
> Below that, usage bars. If you're on the Free plan you can see exactly how many events you're using out of your one allowed. If you're on Pro the bars show operator count and display count versus your limits. These update in real time — as soon as you create an event or invite an operator, the bar adjusts.
>
> This is useful not just for tracking your own usage but for explaining your limits to a client or a team member: 'we're on Free, we have one event — to run the second event we need to upgrade.'"

[Screen: Show the profile panel with usage bars filled at different levels — first showing Free plan with event bar nearly full, then toggling to a Pro plan view showing headroom.]

## UPGRADING — THE BILLING BUTTON (2:20–3:10)

[Screen: Inside the user profile panel, click the Billing button. This opens the Stripe Checkout flow — show the plan selection screen with Pro (€79/mo) and Per-Event (one-time price) options.]

> "The Billing button inside the profile panel launches the Stripe checkout directly. CueDeck uses Stripe for all payment processing — it's the same payment infrastructure used by most major SaaS products, so your card details are handled by Stripe, not stored by CueDeck.
>
> On the checkout screen you'll see the plan options: Pro at €79 per month billed monthly, and Per-Event credit at the current per-event rate for a single-use unlock.
>
> Select your plan, enter your card details, and confirm. The upgrade is applied immediately — your plan badge in the profile panel updates, the usage limits change, and AI features unlock without a page refresh."

[Screen: Show the Stripe checkout form — plan selection, then the card entry fields. Do not enter any card details — show the form layout only and narrate what it contains.]

> "CueDeck also supports VAT invoicing for European businesses. The checkout asks for your billing address and VAT number if applicable — invoices with proper VAT line items are generated automatically by Stripe."

---

## THE CUSTOMER PORTAL (3:10–3:40)

[Screen: Back in the profile panel, show a secondary button or link labelled "Manage Subscription" or accessible via the same Billing flow. Navigate to the Stripe customer portal — show the portal interface: current plan, next billing date, payment method, invoice history.]

> "Once you're on a paid plan, the Manage Subscription link in the profile panel takes you to the Stripe customer portal. From there you can change your payment method, update your billing address, download past invoices as PDFs, or cancel your subscription.
>
> Invoice history is important for production companies billing clients — every monthly charge generates a proper Stripe invoice with a unique number, your billing address, and the line item. Download it, forward it to your accounts department, done."

---

## WHEN TO CHOOSE EACH OPTION (3:40–4:20)

[Screen: Return to the CueDeck console — close any open panels. The session list is visible.]

> "The practical decision tree:
>
> If you're just evaluating CueDeck — start Free. Sign up, create your event, run it, see if the workflow fits. No cost, no commitment.
>
> If you run events regularly — monthly or more often, or multiple events per year with large crews — Pro at €79 a month is straightforward value. The AI agents alone justify it on any professional production.
>
> If you run one or two events a year and don't want a monthly subscription — Per-Event credit is built for you. Buy it when you need it, it unlocks for that event, and you're not paying €79 in the months you're not working.
>
> If you're an event management company, a large AV house, or you need procurement process, SLA, or volume pricing — talk to the Enterprise team. There's a contact link on the pricing page.
>
> The pricing is designed so that the tool pays for itself. One session overrun caught early by the Cue Engine can save thirty minutes of programme time. One well-written client debrief from the Report Generator can win the next contract. The numbers work."
