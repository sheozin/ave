# Episode 05 — The Broadcast Bar: Team-Wide Messaging in One Click
**Duration target:** 4–5 min
**Style:** Show broadcast from director, show receive on two other role windows

---

## INTRO (0:00–0:20)

[Screen: console, broadcast bar visible at top]

> "Group chats during a live event are noise. By the time someone reads a message and replies, the moment has passed. CueDeck's broadcast bar solves this — one message, every connected device, zero round-trips."

---

## HOW IT WORKS (0:20–1:00)

[Screen: zoom in on broadcast bar — the blue bar near the top of the console]

> "The broadcast bar sits at the top of the console. Directors type a message, hit send, and it appears immediately across all connected devices — every stage manager, every AV engineer, every interpretation booth — as a coloured overlay they can't miss.
>
> Messages dismiss when each person taps them. The director can see who's cleared it. It's not a chat — it's a command."

---

## SENDING A BROADCAST (1:00–2:00)

[Screen: director view — broadcast bar input]

> "Let's send one. Click the broadcast bar. Type your message — 'Delay 5 minutes — hold all transitions'. There's a character counter. Keep it short and punchy — this is a live environment.
>
> Hit Send. Watch."

[Action: type message and send, show second browser window (stage view) receiving the banner overlay]

> "Boom. Every connected role sees it instantly. The banner sits at the top of their view until they dismiss it. They can't accidentally miss it scrolling past."

---

## PRESETS (2:00–3:00)

[Screen: show preset buttons under the broadcast bar]

> "For common situations you'll use over and over, CueDeck has broadcast presets. One click — no typing needed.
>
> 'HOLD — Do not proceed.' Great for when you need everything to pause right now.
> 'Speakers to stage immediately.' Self-explanatory.
> 'Running 5 minutes late — adjust your clocks.'
>
> The director can customise these presets for each event in advance. We'll show that in the signage episode."

[Action: click a preset — show it send and appear on second window]

---

## LOCAL DISMISS (3:00–3:30)

[Screen: stage view with broadcast banner, dismiss it]

> "Each person dismisses the message on their own device. The director doesn't need to clear it globally. Local dismiss means everyone works at their own pace once they've acknowledged the instruction.
>
> The broadcast stays visible until dismissed — it doesn't auto-fade."

---

## WRAP (3:30–4:00)

>
