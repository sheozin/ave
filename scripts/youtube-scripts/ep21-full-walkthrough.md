# Episode 21 — Full End-to-End Walkthrough: Running a Real Event on CueDeck
**Duration target:** 12–15 min
**Style:** Cinematic and immersive — this is the capstone. No tutorial narration, no "and now let's look at this feature." You are running a real event in real time and the viewer is watching over your shoulder. The energy is calm but purposeful: a professional in their element. Every action is deliberate. Time pressure is real — reference the clock constantly. Use the event name "Tech Summit 2026" throughout. This episode should feel like a masterclass watched once and referenced forever.

> "First thing every morning: programme check. I need to confirm every session is in place before anyone steps on stage.
>
> Ten sessions. Seven on Main Stage, three on Workshop B. Let me run through them.
>
> Session 1: Registration & Welcome Break — Main Stage — 09:00 to 09:30. PLANNED.
> Session 2: Opening Keynote — Sarah Chen — Main Stage — 09:30 to 10:15. PLANNED.
> Session 3: Product Deep Dive — Alex Morgan — Main Stage — 10:15 to 11:00. PLANNED.
> Workshop B Panel — 10:15 parallel — three sessions, all PLANNED.
> The anchor sessions are in place: Coffee Break at 11:00 and Networking Lunch at 13:00.
> Closing Keynote — 16:00 — anchor. All confirmed."

[Screen: Scroll slowly through the session list — pause briefly on the anchor sessions to show the anchor indicator on their cards. Show the filter bar with "Main Stage" and "Workshop B" room chips.]

> "Programme is clean. No orphaned sessions, no missing speakers. The clock sync indicator in the header is green — we're live with the database."

> "Displays are running. Lobby board is showing the full programme. We're ready to receive the first attendees."

> Emma, we're good. Programme is confirmed. Your first cue is Registration at nine hundred — stand by."

## SESSION 1: REGISTRATION & WELCOME BREAK (3:00–4:30)

[Screen: Clock shows 08:58. Select Session 1 — Registration & Welcome Break. Context panel opens on the right. Press `R` — session transitions to READY. Green badge. READY.]

> "Eight fifty-eight. Two minutes before the published start. Registration goes READY.
>
> Emma sees that on her iPad. Attendees are at the doors."

[Screen: Clock shows 09:01. Context panel still showing Session 1. Click Go Live (or press the keyboard shortcut). Session transitions to LIVE. Red badge. The stage timer display activates.]

> "Nine oh-one. Doors are open, welcome music is on. Session 1 is LIVE.
>
> The stage timer is running. The lobby board has flipped the Registration session to active."

[Screen: Clock shows 09:28. Session 1 is LIVE. Click End Session. Session transitions to ENDED. Grey badge.]

> "Nine twenty-eight — registration wraps two minutes early. Session 1 ENDED. Good start."

## SESSION 2: OPENING KEYNOTE (4:30–7:30)

[Screen: Session 2 — Opening Keynote — Sarah Chen — Main Stage — is now at the top of the active programme. Select it. Press `R`. Session transitions to READY.]

> "Session 2 goes READY at nine twenty-eight. Sarah should be in the wings.
>
> I'll call her to stage."

[Screen: In the context panel, click Call Speaker. Session transitions to CALLING. Orange badge.]

> "CALLING state. Emma sees this on her iPad — that's her cue to walk Sarah to the stage entrance."

[Screen: Press `B` to open broadcast bar. Type "Sarah Chen to Main Stage please — standby for intro". Press Enter. Broadcast sends to all connected devices.]

> "Broadcast sent. Every operator in the room knows where Sarah is and what's happening next."

[Screen: Clock shows 09:32. Session is in CALLING state. Click Go Live. Session transitions to LIVE. Red badge. The stage timer starts counting up from 0:00.]

> "Nine thirty-two. Sarah is on stage, intro is done, she's speaking. Opening Keynote is LIVE.
>
> Planned start was nine-thirty. We're two minutes late — within normal tolerance. The stage timer is running, the confidence monitor on stage shows the session title and time."

[Screen: Clock shows approximately 10:10. Session 2 is LIVE. The Cue Engine modal fires — a blue notification or modal: "Opening Keynote — 8 minutes remaining. Pre-cue: prepare for Product Deep Dive." Show the Cue Engine modal open.]

> "There it is — the Cue Engine. Eight minutes out from the planned end time, it fires automatically. That's my cue to start the pre-cue sequence: confirm the next speaker is ready, confirm AV is set for the handoff.
>
> Dismiss."

[Screen: Click Dismiss on the Cue Engine modal. Return to normal view.]

> "Sarah is still going at ten sixteen — she's running a minute long. I'll nudge the cascade."

[Screen: In the context panel for Session 2, click the +2 delay button (or whatever is available). The delay fires — sessions below in Main Stage shift by 2 minutes. Session 3 (Product Deep Dive) and subsequent Main Stage sessions show +2 min tags.]

> "Two-minute nudge applied. The cascade shifted everything downstream on Main Stage by two minutes. Workshop B is unaffected — those sessions are on their own track."

[Screen: Clock shows 10:17. Session 2 still LIVE. Click End Session. Session transitions to ENDED.]

> "Ten seventeen. Keynote wraps. Sarah gets a round of applause, she's been great. Session 2 ENDED.
>
> We're running two minutes behind planned. The cascade has already adjusted everything — no manual work needed."

## SESSION 3 & PARALLEL WORKSHOP B (7:30–9:00)

[Screen: Session 3 — Product Deep Dive — is now top of the Main Stage queue. Separately, Workshop B Panel is also PLANNED — visible in the session list. Select Session 3. Press `R`. Transitions to READY.]

> "Ten seventeen. Session 3 goes READY. Alex Morgan is in the wings.
>
> Simultaneously, Workshop B Panel is starting on the parallel track."

[Screen: Click the "Workshop B" room filter chip. The list now shows only Workshop B sessions. Select the Workshop B Panel session. Press `R`. Transitions to READY. A second READY badge appears.]

> "Workshop B goes READY. Two simultaneous sessions in pre-cue.
>
> This is the multi-track reality: both tracks are moving at once. I'm keeping the director view and switching between room filters."

[Screen: Clear the room filter (click Workshop B chip again). Both sessions are visible — Session 3 (Main Stage READY) and Workshop B Panel (READY). Click Go Live on Session 3. Transitions to LIVE.]

> "Session 3 — Product Deep Dive — LIVE on Main Stage at ten nineteen. Two minutes behind planned, as expected after the keynote nudge."

[Screen: Click Go Live on the Workshop B Panel session. Transitions to LIVE.]

> "Workshop B Panel LIVE. Both tracks running. The Programme signage board in the lobby is showing both sessions as active — attendees in the corridor can see exactly where to go."

## DELAY SCENARIO (9:00–10:30)

[Screen: Clock shows approximately 11:05. Session 3 (Main Stage) has already ended. Workshop B Panel is still LIVE — running past its planned end of 11:00. The session has an OVERRUN badge — it flipped from LIVE to OVERRUN automatically after the planned end time passed.]

> "It's eleven-oh-five. Workshop B Panel has flipped to OVERRUN — it was supposed to end at eleven hundred. The moderator is still taking questions.
>
> I need to make a decision: cut it now, or let it run and cascade.
>
> I'll let it run to eleven-ten and cascade five minutes."

[Screen: Wait until clock shows 11:10. Workshop B Panel is still in OVERRUN. Apply a +5 min delay from the context panel. The cascade fires — subsequent Workshop B sessions shift by five minutes. The Networking Lunch (anchor session) does NOT shift — it stays at 13:00.]

> "Five-minute delay applied. All subsequent Workshop B sessions have shifted — but the Networking Lunch at thirteen hundred is an anchor. It doesn't move.
>
> The gap before lunch on Workshop B is now slightly tighter. The afternoon sessions on Workshop B will need to move briskly, but the programme is intact."

## BROADCAST (10:30–11:00)

[Screen: Press `B`. The broadcast bar opens. Type "Running approx. 5 minutes late on Workshop B track — schedule updated on lobby displays." Press Enter. Broadcast sends.]

> "Five minutes late on Workshop B. I'm broadcasting to the whole crew — and to anyone on the registration role watching the session queue.
>
> The lobby board has already updated the Workshop B times automatically from the cascade — but the broadcast message gives context. People appreciate the transparency."

[Screen: Show the broadcast banner appearing on a second browser window representing a stage manager's iPad view. Then dismiss it.]

> "The afternoon runs cleanly. The cascade absorbed the Workshop B overrun. Lunch break at thirteen hundred on the dot — the anchor held. The afternoon keynote went live at fifteen-fifty, eight minutes early, wrapped on time.
>
> One by one, sessions end. By seventeen-ten, all ten sessions are ENDED. The Main Stage timer has stopped. The lobby board is showing all sessions as completed.
>
> Tech Summit 2026 is done."

[Screen: Session list — all 10 sessions with grey ENDED badges. Clean. Complete.]

## AI REPORT GENERATOR (12:30–13:30)

[Screen: Navigate to the AI Agents panel in the sidebar. Click the Report Generator module. The Generate Report button is prominent.]

> "The event is over. Let's close it out properly.
>
> I click Generate Report. The modal opens and the thinking animation starts — you can see the five steps working through: analysing session data, processing the log, calculating metrics, drafting the summary, writing recommendations."

[Screen: Show the report generating — thinking animation visible for a few seconds. Then the tabs appear. Click the Summary tab.]

> "Summary tab first. Four stat cards: ten sessions, ninety percent on-time rate, plus one point eight minutes average overrun, zero incidents. Clean day.
>
> The executive summary from Claude reads: 'Tech Summit 2026 ran a highly efficient programme across two tracks with minimal schedule variance. The opening keynote ran two minutes long, absorbed by a standard delay cascade, and a single five-minute overrun on Workshop B was managed without impact to the Networking Lunch anchor. Overall execution reflects strong pre-event preparation and responsive real-time management.'
>
> That goes directly into my client email."

[Screen: Click the Sessions tab. Show the variance table — all sessions listed with planned vs actual times and delta values. Mostly green, two orange rows (the keynote at +2 and the Workshop B panel at +5).]

> "Sessions tab: every session, planned versus actual, delta in minutes. Two orange rows — the keynote nudge and the Workshop B overrun. Everything else is on time or within a minute. That's a very good day."

[Screen: Click the Recommendations tab. Show the AI Narrative paragraph and the numbered recommendations list.]

> "And the recommendations. Claude has flagged three things: consider adding a two-minute buffer to the opening keynote slot in the next Tech Summit iteration, confirm Workshop B moderator briefing on time management, and review the lobby signage placement for better visibility of the Workshop B track timing updates.
>
> Specific. Actionable. Based on what actually happened today."
